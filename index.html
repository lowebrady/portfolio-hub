About Brady

// Who I Am

Researcher.
Designer.
Builder.

I came to UX through a mix of psychology, technology, and a genuine curiosity about why some products feel effortless and others feel like work. At Michigan State, I've built that curiosity into a full practice — running usability studies, auditing for accessibility, conducting qualitative research, and building the interfaces myself in code.

On the analytics side, I completed the Global Technology Experience Data Analysis track, where I worked with real datasets across transportation, e-commerce, and consumer behavior. I believe good analysis isn't just about the numbers — it's about telling the right story to the right people.

What makes me different is that I hold both. I design with data and analyze with empathy. That combination gives me an edge in roles where research, design, and decision-making overlap: UX research ops, product analytics, and customer insights.

Outside of portfolio work, I'm a freelance graphic designer and the VP of Delta Kappa Epsilon Fraternity. I bring the same systems thinking to every project — understand the problem, find the signal in the noise, communicate clearly.
